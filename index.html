HPC + QC: Why Hybrid Matters

Quantum won’t live alone. Workflows will mix classical HPC with QC accelerators — which makes cryogenic reliability mission‑critical.

Shared constraints

  • Job scheduling across cooldown/warm‑up windows
  • Thermal budgets & power envelopes
  • Calibration/retuning as part of pipeline

Observed realities

  • Most failures are gradual, visible in traces first
  • Bottlenecks hide in cryo & I/O, not always the qubit
  • Data residency & security matter for enterprises

What Q‑ARNE adds

  • Health signals exposed to schedulers & SLURM
  • Risk‑aware queueing (pause/route jobs)
  • APIs for observability stacks (Prometheus/Grafana)

Cryogenics 101 — What’s Inside a Dilution Refrigerator?

Key subsystems

  • Pumps & compressors (He circulation)
  • Heat exchangers, still, mixing chamber
  • Filters, wiring, traps (e.g., LN2 trap)

Typical failure modes

  • Blockages, leaks, vacuum degradation
  • Cooldown drifts & unexpected thermal loads
  • Sensor noise & connector issues

Q‑ARNE monitors these signals continuously and flags issues with context — not just alerts.
